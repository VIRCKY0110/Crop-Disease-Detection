Leaf Blight

Symptoms

Leaf blight is characterized by:

  • Yellowing of leaves, wilting, and dark spots.
  • Brown-black leaf spots that grow in target-like concentric rings.
  • As the disease progresses, leaves turn brown, curl up, and die.

Prevention

To prevent leaf blight:

  • Rotate crops to minimize disease buildup in the soil.
  • Maintain proper spacing for air circulation around plants.
  • Use resistant varieties of crops whenever possible.
  • Avoid working in wet fields to reduce the spread of spores.

Treatment

Treatment options include:

  • Use fungicides as needed, particularly during wet conditions.
  • Ensure proper irrigation practices to avoid excess moisture on leaves.
  • Remove and dispose of infected plant debris to reduce sources of infection.

  • Extended periods of leaf wetness due to rain or dew.
  • Prolonged warm and humid weather during the growing season.
